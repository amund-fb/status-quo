# About Friendly Beast

Friendly Beast is an AI-native operating partner to owners and investors in consumer goods brands. Based in Oslo, Norway. We work for PE firms, holding companies, family offices, and founders — helping them make brands bigger while keeping companies smaller.

## The stack

- **Claude** — orchestration layer via MCP (Projects, Skills, Cowork, Code)
- **Notion** — structured knowledge, client workspaces, IP library
- **Google Workspace** — email, calendar, Drive
- **BigQuery** — quantitative data, client metrics, financial pipelines
- **Slack** — async comms via MCP
- **Microsoft Office** — PowerPoint decks, Excel models, Word docs

## What we build for clients

- Automated weekly business reviews (BigQuery → Claude → formatted .docx)
- AI demand/supply forecasting
- Financial and operating models
- Claude Projects per client with brand context and custom Skills
- Leave-behind artifacts (scorecards, dashboards, interactive tools)

## Business model

Monthly flat fee, single invoice, equity/fee hybrid options. Three offerings:

- **Journey** — end-to-end transformation
- **Intervention** — specific problem-solving
- **Rental** — embedded talent on demand

Target: 50M NOK/year boutique with <5 people and 50% operating EBITDA.

## Visual identity

Friendly Beast has a bold, editorial aesthetic. The visual identity is minimal, confident, and premium — built on restrained color use and a striking typographic system. The brand presents itself with the visual authority of a high-end creative agency: the work speaks through restraint and typographic power, not decoration.

### Colors

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Black | `#000000` | Dominant brand color. Primary canvas for presentations and the newsletter. |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | Primary text color on black backgrounds. Logos and display elements. |
| Friendly Beast Green | `#3AE49D` | Signature accent. Used sparingly — data highlights, key stats, selective emphasis. High-impact on black. |
| Yellow | `#FFF766` | Secondary accent in the newsletter for the Stack & MCP section and watchlist items. |
| Dark Gray | `#5E5E5E` | Subtle dividers, muted secondary text, structural elements. |
| Light Gray | `#D5D5D5` | Table borders, secondary backgrounds, muted labels. |

**Color rules:** The palette is intentionally limited. The power comes from restraint. Green is used like a highlighter pen — sparingly, deliberately, and always to draw the eye to what matters most. Never for large areas, headings, or body text. Do NOT introduce additional colors.

### Typography

**Brand typefaces** (for presentations, Word docs, PDFs):

| Typeface | Role | Fallback |
|----------|------|----------|
| Monument Extended Bold | Display headlines, section headers, ALL CAPS titles | Impact, Arial Black |
| Calendas Plus | Subtitles, narrative lead-ins, pull quotes, editorial emphasis | Georgia, Times New Roman |
| KH Teka Medium | Body text, labels, captions, footnotes, all functional text | Helvetica Neue, Arial |

**Newsletter web adaptations** (Google Fonts for universal browser rendering):

| Typeface | Replaces | Role in newsletter |
|----------|----------|--------------------|
| DM Sans | KH Teka Medium + Monument Extended | Headings, labels, section titles, UI text (700 weight, uppercase) |
| Newsreader | Calendas Plus | Body text, card content, editorial voice (400–600 weight) |

The newsletter uses these web fonts because they load in any browser without font installation, while preserving the same hierarchy: geometric sans for structure, serif for editorial content.

### Brand voice & tone

- **Confident and direct.** Short, provocative statements. Questions that challenge.
- **Conversational but authoritative.** Not corporate-speak. Reads like a creative brief, not a boardroom report.
- **Forward-looking and ambitious.** Language of evolution, ambition, and cultural positioning.
- **Minimal.** Fewer words, bigger impact. Let the typography and layout carry weight. Avoid dense paragraphs where a single bold line would suffice.
